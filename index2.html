Epiphone AJ-220SCE

Superb Value and Performance! The Epiphone AJ-220SCE may be the best value in the acoustic/electric guitar industry today. Since the introduction in 1998 of Epiphone's Advanced Jumbo acoustics, the AJ-220SCE carries on the tradition of unequaled quality and performance for far less than you would expect to pay. That Legendary "Advanced Jumbo" Sound! In 1936, Gibson introduced the "Advance Jumbo" (AJ) acoustic guitar. With it's one-inch wider 16" lower bout, rounded or sloped shoulder dreadnought shape and a 25.5" scale length, the AJ was a powerful flat-top referred to in later Gibson literature as an "acoustic cannon." Today, this legendary design is carried over with Epiphone's new AJ-220SCE acoustic guitar..

Yamaha, Acoustic Guitar Fx310 /natural

Unbeatable value has made the dreadnought-style F310 a best seller. The perfect choice for discerning beginners, and also available as an electro-acoustic. The F310 offers outstanding quality at a truly budget price. The lively sound from the spruce/meranti body has a bright, sweet resonance not unlike Yamaha's renowned FGs, and real rosewood is used for the fingerboard and bridge. The handling comfort of the slightly reduced body depth and intermediate scale length will be particularly appreciated by younger players. On the powered FX310 version, the passive preamp system is delightfully simple - just volume and tone and no battery required - yet the sound is realistically acoustic-like, with an excellent tonal balance across the strings. An ideal choice for the first-time electro-acoustic buyer..
